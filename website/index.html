Enterprise governance and architectural judgment

Define and defend the principles that determine whether an enterprise automation program is safe at scale: identity and access, environment lifecycle, separation of duties, integration patterns, audit posture. The decisions that matter rarely reduce to a technology choice; they come down to whether the structure underneath holds under regulatory scrutiny, organizational growth, and ten years of staff turnover. On a Fortune 500 enablement program I redirected the customer's lift-and-shift instinct toward event-driven messaging on long-running workflows, locking in scale before it became a production problem. On a separate migration I caught a structural mismatch between the customer's existing access-control model and the new platform target before it shipped, averting a silent environment-permission failure. Rated above required level on Business Architecture and Systems Thinking in Workato's competency framework.

Agentic AI and the security perimeter around it

Core contributor to Workato's enterprise MCP best-practice corpus and named in the October 2025 launch of the industry's first enterprise-grade Model Context Protocol platform for AI agents. Authored the Virtual User Authentication framework, which addresses the central enterprise question for agentic workflows: how an agent acts as the right user against downstream systems without becoming the customer's most over-permissioned identity. Led the firm's first agentic-AI advisory engagement and translate emerging agent capability into patterns enterprises can deploy under existing security and compliance posture.
